This week's Podcast is about
"Gajendravaradhar
Thirukovil,Kapisthalam".


This
divya Kshetram can be reached
3 miles from Papanasam Railway Station . Kapisthalam is situated
three km from
Papanasam on the Thanjavur-Kumbakonam route via Tiruvaiyaru,
between the rivers
Kollidam and Cauvery. The temple is rather small with a single
prakaram and a
Rajagopuram.
The
Moolavar is found in the Bhujanga Sayanam pose under the
Kanagakruthi Vimanam.
Besides the Gajendra Pushkarani there is another holy water source
known as
Kapila Theertham. The Goddess, Ramamani Thayar, is worshipped in a
separate
shrine to the right of the main sanctum sanctorum. She is also known by
the pure Tamil name of Potramaraiyaal. There are shrines for Yoga
Narasimhar,
Sudarsana, Garuda and the Azhwars.

Moolavar : GajendraVaradhar
Thayar - Ramamanavalli -Potramaraiyal

Theertham  Gajendra Pushkarani,Kapila Theertham
Vimanam -- Kanagakrutha vimanam
Thirumanzhisaiazhwar
 2431  (Pasuram no as in nalayira Divya prabhandam)
Darshan
Time : 6.30am  12, 5-7.30pm







The
legend has it that the temple is located at the spot where
Gajendra,the king of
elephants was caught by a crocodile.Lord Vishnu responded to the
cry of
Gajendra and saved him from the clutches of crocodile.
Vaali
and sugriva  the vanara kings are believed to have worshipped at
this shrine
and hence the name kapisthalam.This Divya desam is closely
associated to
anjaneyar.Gajendra
Moksham is enacted during the tamil month of Panguni.
