On 8.10.2007 in Dinamalar (Thanjai Edn) it is reported :

Press release by Kudavoil Dr. Balasubramanian::

Airavatheswarar Temple in Darasuram was built by Rajaraja II ( AD
1150) which is declared as World Heritage Monument by the UNESCO. The
Front Mandapam is known as RAJA GAMBEERAN THIRUMANDAPAM in the form a
charriot. Underneath the BALIPEEDAM there is one SIMHA PEEDAM which
is sligtly damaged. Its Speciality:: On all the 4 corners, 4 lions
are in sitting posture with their mouth wide-open. The stone as well
the Lions resemble Cambodia / Ankorwatt Lions.

Cambodia King Harsha Varman gave a gift of a stone to RR II 's great-
grand father which was positioned opposite to Nataraja Sabha, as per
Chidambaram inscription.

It is possible that this SIMHA PEEDAM may be of that gift by the
Cambodian King.! a Photo also furnished.
