SUJATHA-ANOTHER KALKI
 
I know Sujatha through my Srirangam sources. ‘Ambi’ (alias Sriman Venkataraman) a famous quote ofSujatha, in his Srirangam articles, a childhood friend of Sujatha, is father of my friend Priya, eminent Bharatanatya Dancer from Srirangam Natyalaya. Though I have met Mr. Rangarajan twice at BEL, Bangalore on some other official assignments, this contact of Ambi, I did not disclose to him. (Laterwhen I met him at Mylapore, I did tell him about this too).
 
When Vamsadhara was published, as per the request of Ambi, (Ambi told me that if he recommended Sujatha to read my book, he will never read and Sujatha will not oblige too for such requests) I have sent the book for his perusal and I had requested him to commend few words and write in popular commercial magazine. The next week itself, Sujatha wrote in AV edition (21-03-2003) with whole lot of words of praise, and even suggested me to go for Telugu translation, as the story was fully dealing with Telugu land. After this incident, I had met him twice in different occasion before my second book, Thirumalai Thirudan was released. And I told him that I would put his comment on Vamsadhara in the back cover of TT for which, he smiled away.
 
But I was not alone in the crowd. Sujatha gave such pat to every writer whoever he comes across. Countless poets and writers got his highly encourage comments.  I used to ask some in-depth meaning on Azhwar prabhandams through mails for which he responds positively. He used to impress with his crisp writings and deep knowledge on the verses.  His knowledge on Thiruvaimozhi and Mudhalaazhwars, somewhat incomparable. Apart from Bhakthi and deeper meanings, he enjoyed the Tamil words
 
For Thirumalai Thirudan, just I have sent the book and after a month I have called him to ask about the book. This time I did not request him to write, but he himself opened the topic, appreciating the work and my free will to enter into a controversial subject. (I know that time he did receive some adverse comments from some Vaishnavite quarters for his writings in some magazine).
 
To my surprise, he said that he has seen also the good comments of Dr.Oppiliappan Koil Sadagopan, on my book, in the Srivaishnavite e-magazine. Sujatha commented people like him cannot write such controversial subjects, as it will create furors all over, andeven advised me to tap such details for my future works. I took it as great compliments from a great scholar.
 
Who can forget his Ganesh and Vasanths? His ‘geno’, or his ‘karaiyellaam shenbagappu’.Saavi rightly said that he would publish whatever Sujatha gives, including his handwriting bill of dirty cloths given to Washer man. His sense of comedy and satiric comment, novelty and revolution, all his wealth of letters. Even in writing ‘adults only jokes’ too he established his stamp. One particular joke on donkey, the end part, I think, he has not revealed at all. From electronics and science to social and historic novels, from dramas to dancefestival reviews, every thing he has written. Apart from writing, he was very great reader too. After Kalki, in the Tamil world, the great Sujatha only did write on all sorts of subjects.
 
Sujatha was already a very famous faceamong the reader’s crowd, but cinema’s like Anniyan, Boys and Sivaji took him somewhere to very great heights. To add with, the friendship of Kamal for long long time and his close touch with tinsel world forthe past thirty years (I think, Priya was first movie, or may be another one in which Jaishankar acted in the roll of Ganesh, or Sridevi acted in Black and white movie – I forgot), made him really unreachable to ordinary people like me. Yet, the simplicity of Sujatha and grace of God, allowed me too to reach him in the right time.
 
KALKIYIN INNORU SAKAAPTHAM MUDIVUKKU VANTHULLATHU.
 
Dhivakar
