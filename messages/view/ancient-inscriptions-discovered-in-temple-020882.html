Ancient inscriptions discovered in temple



Staff Reporter



They relate to details of donations









rare FIND: An early period inscription at the Sri Choleeswarar
temple at Parambur village in Pudukottai district.

PUDUKOTTAI: Ancient inscriptions dating back to the 11th century
have been unearthed at the Rajendra Chozha Keraliswaran temple in
Parambur village near Kudumiyanmalai in the district.

The inscriptions, deciphered by a team of epigraphists, relate to
minute details of donations made by pious persons and devotees.

Some of them contained only a couple of lines engrossed on a big
slab.

The shape of letters of the inscriptions and their correlation with
known historical facts testified that the inscriptions related to
the works executed at the temple, which is in a dilapidated
condition now.

Epigraphist Karu. Rajendran, and curator of Government Museum C.
Govindaraj led the team.
